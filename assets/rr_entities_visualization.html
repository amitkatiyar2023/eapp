Victim A D/O Not Mention Not Mention (Madhya Pradesh) .....Respondents (By Ms. Hemlata Kshatriya - Panel Lawyer) PREAMBLE This appeal coming on for admission this day, the court passed the following: ORDER This is first Criminal Appeal under Section 14-A of the Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes (Prevention of Atrocities)1989, for grant of bail filed on behalf of appellant against the order dated 14.05.2024 passed in B.A. No.200304/2024. The appellant has been arrested in connection with (FIR) / Crime No.151/2024 registered at Police Station- Kotwali, District - Betul for offences 1punishable under Sections 294, 323, 506, 34, 342, 327, 365, 368 of IPC and Section 3(1)(e), 3(1)(da), 3(1)(dha), 3(2)(v) of the Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes (Prevention of Atrocities) Act. As per prosecution story, the allegation against the applicant is that he alongwith co-accused demanded money from the complainant, abused him and stripped him of his clothes, tied his hands an legs with a rope in a naked state and assaulted him with spade and belt and made his video viral. Thereafter, complainant lodged report in Police Station Damoh. Learned counsel for the appellant submitted that the appellant is innocent and he has falsely been implicated in the case. RLC FIR has been lodged after three months. RLC In MLC no injury has been found. FAC It is also contended that the appellant is in custody since 14.02.2024 and charge-sheet has been filed. FAC In view of the aforesaid, prayer has been made to enlarge the appellant on bail. RPC Learned Panel Lawyer for the respondent/State has opposed the prayer for grant of bail. Looking overall facts and circumstances of the case, I deem it to be a fit case to release the appellant on bail. RATIO Therefore, without commenting on the merit of the case, this appeal is allowed. RPC It is directed that the appellant shall be released on bail on his furnishing a personal bond in a sum of Rs.40,000/- (Rupees Forty Thousand only) with one surety in the like amount to the satisfaction of the trial Court concerned for his regular appearance before the said Court on all such dates as may be fixed in this regard during pendency of trial. RPC It is directed that the appellant shall abide by the conditions enumerated under Section 437(3) of Cr.P.C. Accordingly, this appeal stands allowed and disposed of. RPC 2 ( PRAMOD KUMAR AGRAWAL )
